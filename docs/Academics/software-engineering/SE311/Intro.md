## Course Chapters & Lesson Overview
This course explores how software requirements are discovered, analyzed, documented, validated, and managed. Students learn elicitation techniques, modeling methods, documentation standards, and how to manage evolving requirements in real projects.

### Chapter 1: Basics of Requirements Engineering
- What requirements are and why they matter
- Types of requirements (functional, non-functional, constraints)
- Requirements engineering process
- Main activities in RE

### Chapter 2: Documentation Standards
- IEEE 830-1998 standard
- Relationship to ISO/IEC 12207
- Writing high-quality requirement documents

### Chapter 3: Requirements Inception & Problem Analysis
- Understanding the problem domain
- Business requirements
- Vision and scope documents
- Project viability analysis

### Chapter 4: Requirements Elicitation
- Goals, risks, and challenges
- Sources of requirements
- Elicitation tasks and techniques
- Interviews, workshops, observation, prototyping

### Chapter 5: Requirements Analysis & Specification
- Stages of analysis
- Methods and tools
- Structuring and refining requirements

### Chapter 6: Requirements Modeling (UML)
- Data modeling concepts
- Scenario-based modeling (Use Case Diagrams)
- Class-based modeling (classes, objects, attributes, operations)
- CRC models and collaboration diagrams

### Chapter 7: Requirements Modeling (Continued)
- Activity diagrams
- State charts
- Behavioral modeling

### Chapter 8: Goal Modeling & GRL Workshop
- URN and GRL frameworks
- Modeling stakeholder goals and motivations

### Chapter 9: Requirements Verification & Validation
- Ensuring correctness, consistency, completeness
- Validation techniques
- Negotiation with stakeholders

### Chapter 10: Requirements Prioritization & Negotiation
- Prioritization methods (MoSCoW, Kano, etc.)
- Handling conflicts and trade-offs

### Chapter 11: Requirements Prioritization (Continued)
- Refining scope and release planning

### Chapter 12: Requirements Management
- Traceability
- Baselines
- Change management
- Requirements management tools (e.g., DOORS)

### Chapter 13: Requirements in Development Processes
- Requirements in Agile methods
- Requirements across software process frameworks
- Final project presentations
