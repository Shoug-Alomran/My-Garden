# SE311 - Software Requirements Engineering

SE311 covers software requirements for different types of software systems. The
course focuses on discovering and eliciting requirements, documentation
standards, languages and models for representing requirements, analysis and
validation techniques, need/goal/use-case analysis, quality requirements, and
requirements management through change, traceability, and feature interaction.

## Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of SE311, students should be able to:

1. Recognize requirement types and the importance of software requirement
   analysis.
2. Select and use requirements elicitation techniques for different stakeholder
   situations.
3. Analyze requirements documentation for high-level requirements engineering.
4. Develop requirement models using goals, use cases, UML class/activity
   diagrams, and state charts.
5. Evaluate requirements through validation, negotiation, priority, and scope
   techniques.
6. Measure requirements quality using accuracy, unambiguity, consistency, and
   completeness criteria.
7. Manage requirements as they change through releases and product lines.
8. Work in a group to develop project requirements and documentation.

## Course Content

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   **Basics of Requirements Engineering**

    ---

    Definitions, importance of requirements, requirement types, the
    requirements engineering process, and main activities.

-   **Documentation Standards**

    ---

    IEEE 830-1998 and the relationship between IEEE 830 and ISO/IEC 12207.

-   **Requirements Inception and Problem Analysis**

    ---

    Business requirements, problem analysis steps, vision and scope documents,
    and project viability.

-   **Requirements Elicitation**

    ---

    Elicitation goals, risks, challenges, sources of requirements, tasks,
    problems, and techniques.

-   **Requirements Analysis and Specification**

    ---

    Analysis stages, analysis methods, tools, and specification preparation.

-   **Requirements Modeling**

    ---

    Data modeling, scenario-based modeling, use case diagrams, class-based
    modeling, CRC models, collaboration diagrams, activity diagrams, and state
    charts.

-   **Goal Modeling**

    ---

    URN and GRL goal modeling with workshop-style practice.

-   **Requirements Verification and Validation**

    ---

    Checking correctness, consistency, completeness, feasibility, and agreement
    with stakeholder needs.

-   **Requirements Prioritization and Negotiation**

    ---

    Prioritizing requirements, managing scope, and negotiating trade-offs.

-   **Requirements Management**

    ---

    Traceability, baselines, change management, requirements management tools,
    and DOORS-style tool practice.

-   **Requirements in Development Processes**

    ---

    Requirements approaches in software process engineering frameworks and
    agile methods, with project presentations.

</div>

## Study Path

1. Start with requirement types, the RE process, and documentation standards.
2. Study inception, problem analysis, and elicitation before modeling.
3. Practice use case, class, activity, state, data, and goal models.
4. Move into validation, prioritization, negotiation, and management.
5. Use the cheat sheets, quizzes, UML guide, and mindmap for final review.
