## Course Chapters & Lesson Overview
This course builds the mathematical foundation required for computing.
Students learn logic, sets, functions, number theory, counting, and
proof techniques used throughout computer science.

### 1. The Foundations: Logic, Sets, and Functions
- Propositional logic and truth tables
- Applications of logic in computing
- Logical equivalences
- Predicates and quantifiers
- Rules of inference and formal reasoning

### 2. Basic Structures: Sets, Functions, Sequences, Sums
- Set definitions and operations
- Functions: injective, surjective, bijective
- Sequences and summations
- Matrices and their applications

### 3. Number Theory and Cryptography
- Integers, divisibility, modular arithmetic
- Primes and the Euclidean algorithm
- Introduction to classical cryptography

### 4. Induction and Recursion
- Mathematical induction
- Recursive definitions
- Structural induction

### 5. Counting
- Basic counting rules
- Permutations and combinations
- Binomial coefficients

### 6. Advanced Counting Techniques
- Recurrence relations
- Solving recurrences

### 7. Relations
- Properties of relations
- Representations (matrices, graphs)
- Closures and equivalence relations
