# :balance_scale: ETHCS 303 - Ethics and Cybersecurity

ETHCS 303 focuses on how ethical reasoning applies to computing,
cybersecurity, privacy, law, professional responsibility, and real technical
decision-making. The course is not only about memorizing definitions. A major
part of it is learning how to explain whether an action is ethical, legal,
responsible, risky, or professionally unacceptable.

This page is the course-level overview. When the official syllabus is available,
this page can be updated with the exact weekly schedule, assessment dates,
grading breakdown, and instructor-specific expectations.

---

## What You Will Learn

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

- Explain core ethical concepts, moral systems, and major ethical theories.
- Apply ethical theories such as Kantianism, utilitarianism, and social
  contract theory to computing scenarios.
- Analyze privacy issues in cyberspace, cloud computing, social media, mobile
  systems, and connected technologies.
- Recognize ethical, legal, and security issues in cybersecurity and networked
  systems.
- Understand the role of Saudi cyber law in addressing cybercrime and digital
  misconduct.
- Evaluate professional responsibilities in software engineering, systems
  analysis, system administration, and security work.
- Explain social engineering attacks and safer practices for individuals and
  organizations.
- Discuss business ethics, organizational codes of ethics, whistleblowing, and
  professional-client relationships.
- Write stronger case-study answers using ethical reasoning, relevant concepts,
  and clear justification.

## :calendar: Course Flow

The exact timing will be updated once the syllabus is received. For now, the
material is organized in a practical study order:

| Phase | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1 | Foundations: ethical hacking, privacy, network security, and basic cyber risks |
| 2 | Ethical reasoning: moral systems, Kantianism, utilitarianism, and social contract theory |
| 3 | Law and responsibility: Saudi cyber law, professional codes, and software engineering ethics |
| 4 | Applied issues: business ethics, social engineering, social media, and organizational dilemmas |
| 5 | Exam preparation: past-exam style questions, written scenarios, quizzes, and model answers |
